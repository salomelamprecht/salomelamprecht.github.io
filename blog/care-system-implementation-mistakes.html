Common Mistakes When Implementing a New Care System

March 2026  |  Salome Lamprecht

Implementing a new care management or rostering system is exciting. It promises better visibility, less admin, and smoother operations.

But in my experience of implementing systems across dozens of care branches, the same mistakes come up time and time again, and they are almost always avoidable.

1. Rushing the data migration

The data you bring across from your old system is the foundation of everything. If it is messy, incomplete, or incorrectly mapped, your new system will cause more problems than it solves from day one. Take the time to clean and validate your data before you go live, not after.

2. Going live without proper training

Switching on a system and hoping staff figure it out is a recipe for workarounds, errors, and frustration. Your office staff, your field carers, and your managers all use the system differently and they each need appropriate support. A one-size-fits-all training session is rarely enough.

3. Not configuring the system to your workflows

Every care organisation works slightly differently. A system that has not been configured to reflect how your team actually operates will be fought against rather than embraced. Time spent on configuration before go-live saves a great deal of time and frustration afterwards.

4. Forgetting about reporting

Most care providers focus on getting the operational side working and treat reporting as an afterthought. But if your reports are not set up correctly from the start, you will be making decisions based on incomplete or inaccurate data. Good reporting should be part of the implementation plan, not something bolted on at the end.
